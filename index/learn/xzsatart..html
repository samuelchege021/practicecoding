Cost-Benefit Analysis (CBA) is a methodical process for determining if a project, investment, or policy choice is economically feasible. It entails weighing the advantages and disadvantages of a certain course of action to see if the advantages exceed the disadvantages. In business, government, and economics, CBA is frequently utilized to help in decision-making about the distribution of resources.
